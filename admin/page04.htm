"SKILL WITH PEOPLE FOR CARRIER SUCCESS & HAPPY LIFE"

Understanding People and Human Nature:

People are primarily interested in themselves and not in you!

Putting this same thought another way:
The other person is ten thousand times more interested in himself than he is in you.

And vice versa! You are interested more in yourself than you are in any other person in the world.


How to skillfully talk to people:

Take these four words out of your vocabulary - “I, me my, mine”.

Substitute for those four words with one word - “You”
which is the most powerful word spoken by the human tongue.


How to skillfully make people feel important:

1. Listen to them

2. Applaud and compliment them

3.Use their names and pictures as often as possible

4. Pause before you answer them

5. Use their words - “You” and “Your”

6. Pay attention to everybody in a group


How to skillful listen to people:

Look at the person who is talking and try not interrupt suddenly.

Lean to the words of the speaker and listen intently.

Ask questions politely, sometimes it helps.

Stick to speaker’s subject and do not try to change the core interest.

Use the speakers words-“You” and “Your”.


HOW TO SKILLFULLY CRITIQUE PEOPLE:

Criticism should be made in absolute privacy, this is most important.

Preface criticism with a kind word or compliment.

Make the criticism impersonal-criticism the act, not the person

Ask for cooperation, do not demand it.

One criticism to an offence.

Finish the criticism on a friendly note.


How to skillfully thank people:

When you say “thank you”, MEAN it. Be sincere when you thank people.

Say it clearly and distinctly.

When thanking people, don’t mumble, whisper or slur the words.

Say thanks AS if you are glad you are saying it. Say it clearly and distinctly.

Look at the people you thank. Thank people by name.

Work at thanking people so often to improve the skill.


HOW TO SKILLFULLY MAKE A GOOD IMPRESSION:

Be sincere. Show enthusiasm. Don’t be overanxious.

Don’t try to build yourself up by running other people down.

Don’t knock anybody or anything.


How to skillfully make a talk:

Know what you want to say.

Look at the audience while you talk.

Talk about what the audience is interested in.

Don’t try to make a speech.

Knowledge itself is of no value, it's the use of knowledge that makes it valuable.

Putting this thought another way:
Life does not pay off for you on what you can do, life pays off for you on what you do.

This knowledge is your key to a better life, more friends, more success, and more happiness.
